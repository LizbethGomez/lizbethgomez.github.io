Who am I?

NYC 2022

I’m currently pursuing my doctoral degree at Drexel University’s Dornsife School of Public Health in Philadelphia, and I am also a GATHER Fellow. Under the guidance of Dr. Jane Clougherty, my research focuses on evaluating how social and environmental factors impact the efficacy of pharmaceutical treatments. Prior to this, I earned a Master’s in Public Health with a concentration in Environmental Health Science from Mailman School of Public Health and a Bachelor’s of Science from the City College of New York.

My research interests are centered around epidemiological and biostatistical methodologies, with a particular focus on disparities in asthma outcomes. These interests originated from my work as a fellow in the Puerto Rico IPE Service Learning Fellowship where I collaborated with a diverse team to develop a community-based participatory and service learning research project in San Juan. Since then, my passion for producing rigorous and reproducible research has continued to grow.

When I’m not engaged in research, I enjoy indulging my love of food by exploring new culinary experiences. In my free time, I also enjoy gardening, cuddling with my Bichon-Frise named Lilu, baking, reading (including fiction), and coding in R.
